- **Recommended for:** **NEW PLAYERS START HERE**

### Configuration Options Explained

#### Galaxy Size
- **Small (4 planets):** Faster games, less expansion
- **Medium (5 planets):** Balanced gameplay (recommended)
- **Large (6 planets):** Epic campaigns, more strategy

#### Difficulty
- **Easy:** AI gets -20% resources and military
  - Good for learning
  - AI makes more mistakes
  - More forgiving early game
  
- **Normal:** Standard challenge
  - AI plays optimally
  - Fair resource competition
  - Recommended for most players
  
- **Hard:** AI gets +20% bonuses
  - Very challenging
  - AI expands faster
  - Requires perfect play

#### AI Personality

**Aggressive (Commander Kratos):**
- Prioritizes military from turn 1
- Attacks early (turns 10-15)
- High risk, high reward style
- **Counter:** Strong defenses early

**Balanced (General Nexus):**
- Adapts to your playstyle
- Mixed economy and military
- Most unpredictable
- **Counter:** Stay flexible

**Defensive (Overseer Aegis):**
- Builds many defenses
- Rarely attacks first
- Accumulates resources safely
- **Counter:** Economic warfare, siege

**Economic (Magistrate Midas):**
- Focuses on expansion first
- Military comes later
- Snowballs if left alone
- **Counter:** Early military pressure

### ACTION PHASE (This is YOUR turn)

This is where you make ALL your decisions. The game WAITS for you.

#### What You Can Do:

**A) PLANET MANAGEMENT**

1. Click any of your planets
2. See planet details panel
3. Options available:
   - Build new structures
   - Adjust tax rate
   - Commission platoons
   - View/manage buildings
   - Scrap buildings (50% refund)

**B) SPACECRAFT MANAGEMENT**

1. Click planet with docking bay
2. Click [SPACECRAFT] button
3. Purchase options:
   - Battle Cruiser
   - Cargo Cruiser
   - Solar Satellite
   - Atmosphere Processor

**C) FLEET MOVEMENT**

1. Click planet with spacecraft
2. Select spacecraft to move
3. Click destination planet
4. Fleet travels automatically next turn

**D) MILITARY COMMISSIONING**

1. Click your planet
2. Click [PLATOONS] button
3. Configure:
   - Troop count (1-200)
   - Equipment level
   - Weapon type
4. Training begins (10 turns to 100%)

**E) COMBAT PREPARATION**

1. Load platoons onto Battle Cruisers
2. Move fleets to enemy planets
3. Combat resolves in Combat Phase

**F) RESEARCH** (if available)

1. Access research menu
2. Upgrade weapon tiers
3. Cost: 500k+ Credits
4. Takes multiple turns

## ğŸ“ Strategy Tips

### Early Game (Turns 1-10)

**Priority 1: Economy**
- Build 2Ã— Mining Stations
- Build 2Ã— Horticultural Stations
- Get resource production flowing

**Priority 2: Expansion**
- Build 1Ã— Atmosphere Processor
- Start colonizing neutral planet (turn 5)
- Choose Volcanic or Tropical for specialization

**Priority 3: Military Prep**
- Commission platoons turn 5-7 (will train during expansion)
- Build 1Ã— Docking Bay
- Save resources for Battle Cruisers

**DON'T:**
- Build military turn 1 (too expensive, wastes economy)
- Ignore food (starvation spirals are devastating)
- Set tax above 60% (morale penalties hurt growth)

### Mid Game (Turns 11-20)

**Priority 1: Military**
- Complete Battle Cruiser construction
- Embark trained platoons
- Scout enemy strength

**Priority 2: Consolidation**
- Finish colonizing neutral planets
- Build defenses at border planets
- Ensure positive resource income

**Priority 3: Preparation**
- Build second wave of military (while first attacks)
- Research weapon upgrades if possible
- Stockpile resources for extended war

**DON'T:**
- Attack before platoons reach 70%+ training
- Send single Battle Cruiser against fortified planets
- Leave homeworld undefended while invading

### Late Game (Turns 21-30)

**Priority 1: Conquest**
- Systematic planet capture
- Cut off enemy resource production
- Overwhelm with superior numbers

**Priority 2: Defense**
- Defend newly captured planets
- Don't overextend
- Maintain supply lines

**Priority 3: Finishing**
- Locate enemy's last planet
- Prepare overwhelming force
- Execute final assault

**DON'T:**
- Get complacent (enemy can recover quickly)
- Ignore enemy counter-attacks
- Forget to garrison captured planets

## ğŸ¯ Common Beginner Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Building Military Too Early

**What happens:**
- Turn 1: Build Battle Cruiser (50k Cr)
- Turn 2: Broke, can't build economy
- Turn 5: Enemy has 3 planets, you have 1
- Turn 10: Overwhelmed by enemy production

**Solution:**
- Build economy first (turns 1-5)
- Military second (turns 5-10)
- Attack third (turns 15+)

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Food Production

**What happens:**
- Population grows from 500 to 800
- Food consumption: 400/turn
- Food production: 100/turn
- Deficit: -300/turn
- Turn 8: Food hits zero
- STARVATION: Population crashes, morale collapses

**Solution:**
- 1 Horticultural Station per 200 population
- Build food infrastructure BEFORE population grows
- Monitor food levels every turn

### Mistake 3: Attacking Without Training

**What happens:**
- Commission platoons turn 10
- Training: 0%
- Immediately embark and attack
- Strength: 0 (0% training = 0Ã— multiplier)
- Enemy with 50% training: 97.5 strength
- You: Obliterated

**Solution:**
- Commission platoons turn 5
- Wait until turn 15 (100% training)
- THEN attack

### Mistake 4: Single Planet Over-Development

**What happens:**
- Homeworld: 5/5 buildings, 3/3 docking bays
- Other planets: Empty
- Enemy: Expanding to neutrals
- Turn 15: Enemy has 4 planets, you have 1
- Turn 20: Overwhelmed by production

**Solution:**
- Colonize neutrals ASAP
- Spread buildings across planets
- Specialize planets by type

## â“ FAQ: Gameplay Questions

### "How do I know which planet to attack first?"

**Check these factors:**

1. **Distance:** Closer = faster to reach
2. **Defenses:** Look for:
   - No Orbital Defense Platforms (easier)
   - Fewer docked spacecraft
   - Smaller garrison
3. **Resources:** Rich planets are better prizes
4. **Strategic value:** Cut off enemy expansion

**Best targets:**
- Newly colonized planets (minimal defense)
- Volcanic worlds (resource denial)
- Isolated planets (hard for enemy to reinforce)

### "When should I upgrade weapon tiers?"

**Weapon Upgrade Timing:**

- **Missile** (500k Cr, 5 turns):
  - When: Turn 15-20
  - If: You have economic dominance
  - Effect: 1.5Ã— damage in all combats
  
- **Photon Torpedo** (1M Cr, 8 turns):
  - When: Turn 25+
  - If: Long game, need edge for final push
  - Effect: 2.0Ã— damage (doubles fleet power)

**Usually NOT worth it** because:
- Very expensive
- Takes many turns
- Better to build more ships
- Only helps if battles are close

**Exception:** If you're in stalemate and evenly matched

### "How do I defend against enemy attacks?"

**Defensive Strategy:**

**Layer 1: Early Warning**
- Scouts detect enemy fleet movements
- React before they arrive

**Layer 2: Orbital Defense**
- 2Ã— Orbital Defense Platforms = +40% defense
- Makes attacks much costlier

**Layer 3: Fleet Defense**
- Keep Battle Cruisers in orbit
- React to threats

**Layer 4: Garrison**
- Always have platoons on planet
- 2-3 platoons minimum for border worlds

**If enemy is overwhelming:**
- Evacuate valuable units
- Let planet fall
- Counter-attack their weak point

### "What if I run out of resources?"

**Resource Recovery:**

**Out of Credits:**
1. Increase tax rate (temporary spike)
2. Capture enemy planet (50% of their stockpile)
3. Wait for tax revenue accumulation
4. Don't build anything expensive

**Out of Minerals:**
1. Build more Mining Stations
2. Focus on Volcanic planets (5Ã— bonus)
3. Scrap unused buildings (50% refund)
4. Use Cargo Cruisers to transport from rich planets

**Out of Food (CRISIS):**
1. Build Horticultural Station IMMEDIATELY
2. Lower tax rate (helps morale during crisis)
3. Expect population to drop 5% per turn
4. Takes 3-5 turns to recover

**Out of Fuel:**
1. Build Mining Stations (also produce fuel)
2. Limit fleet movements
3. Volcanic planets give 3Ã— fuel

### "Can I win without combat?"

**No.** Victory requires capturing all enemy planets.

**However:** You can minimize combat by:
1. Overwhelming force (enemy surrenders undefended planets)
2. Economic warfare (blockade, cut resources)
3. Waiting for AI mistakes

But eventually you must invade and conquer.
